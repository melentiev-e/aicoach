## Phase Detail

### Phase 1 — Base / Aerobic (Weeks 1–6, Jun 8–Jul 19)

**Goal:** Rebuild aerobic base, establish training rhythm, polarize intensity, introduce LT work, address injury-prevention deficit.

**Weekly structure (6 days):**
- 4–5 × Easy runs (5:15–5:50/km, HR < 148)
- 1 × Threshold session (cruise intervals: 2×3 → 3×3 → 4×3 km @ 4:05–4:10/km)
- 1 × Long run (easy, 20–24 km, HR < 155)
- Add 6×100m strides 2× per week (attached to easy runs)

**Key milestones:**
- ~~By wk 6: 3×3 km @ 4:05/km; LT → 3:50–3:52/km~~ → **EXCEEDED at Wk 4:** threshold 3×3k @ 3:55–3:59/km; Garmin LT 3:38/km as of Jul 6
- Revised Wk 5–6 target: 4×3 km @ 3:45–3:52/km; LT hold at 3:35–3:40/km; shin-free
- Long run progresses 21 → 22 (Wk 5) → 24 km (Wk 6)

**Injury-prevention protocol (start immediately):**
- 3× per week: calf raises (3×20 single-leg eccentric), tibialis anterior raises
- Monitor: any shin soreness > 2/10 → cut next run, report before the threshold session
- No two consecutive hard-surface quality sessions

**Recovery week (Wk 4, Jul 5–11):**
- Volume ~68 km; drop threshold session to 1×3 km; long run 18 km

### Phase 2 — Strength / Threshold (Weeks 7–10, Jul 20–Aug 16)

**Goal:** Drive LT pace from ~3:52 toward 3:42–3:45/km; increase sustainable aerobic load; introduce a second weekly quality session.

**Weekly structure:**
- 3–4 × Easy runs
- 1 × Threshold session (4×3 km or 5×2 km cruise intervals; or 6–8 km continuous tempo)
- 1 × Second quality (hill repeats 6–10×200m, or progression run, or 5×1 km @ 3:45–3:50/km)
- 1 × Long run (24–28 km easy, or last 5 km at MP effort)

**Key milestones:**
- By wk 10: 5×2 km cruise intervals at 3:38–3:44/km (true LT effort) with HR < 174; LT target → 3:30–3:35/km
- First progression long run with MP feel final 5 km (target MP ~3:52–3:58/km at wk 8 fitness; approach 3:46 by wk 14)

**Recovery week (Wk 8, Aug 2–8):**
- Volume ~72 km; single quality session; long run 22 km

### Phase 3 — Specific / Race Prep (Weeks 11–16, Aug 17–Oct 4)

**Goal:** Build marathon-specific fitness; marathon shape from ~45% to 75%+; long runs with MP sections; VO2max ceiling.

**Weekly structure:**
- 2–3 × Easy runs
- 1 × MP session (examples: 3×5 km @ 3:46; 12–16 km with 8–10 km @ 3:46; or 20 km split easy/MP)
- 1 × VO2max or threshold sharpener (3–4 × 1 km @ 3:30–3:38/km; or 2×4 km threshold)
- 1 × Long run (26–34 km, with 8–16 km at MP in final weeks)

**Progression of MP work:**
- Wk 11–12: MP feel only (3:50–3:55/km), learn the effort
- Wk 13–14: 3×5 km @ 3:46/km with 2 min jog
- Wk 15 (peak): 10–12 km continuous @ 3:46–3:50/km within long run
- Wk 16: recovery — back off to MP feel only

**Long run caps:**
- Wk 13: 30 km (easy)
- Wk 14: 32 km (final 8 km @ MP)
- Wk 15: 34 km (final 10 km @ MP) — only if shin-free and TSB > −10

**Recovery week (Wk 12, Aug 30–Sep 5):**
- Volume ~78 km; no VO2max work; long run 24 km easy

**Recovery week (Wk 16, Sep 28–Oct 4):**
- Volume ~86 km; reduce to one quality session; long run 26 km easy — final big-fitness check before taper

### Phase 4 — Taper (Weeks 17–20, Oct 5–Nov 1)

**Goal:** Arrive at start line fresh, legs bouncy, confidence intact.

**Week 17 (Oct 5–11):** ~75 km — 1× MP tune-up (2×6 km @ 3:46), long run 28 km easy
**Week 18 (Oct 12–18):** ~58 km — 1× sharpener (4×1 km @ 3:30), long run 22 km with MP miles
**Week 19 (Oct 19–25):** ~42 km — 1× MP strides (10×200m @ 3:40), easy running only, long run 16 km
**Week 20 (Oct 26–Nov 1):** ~20 km — 2–3 easy runs + race (Nov 1)

**Taper rules:**
- Do not add sessions "because I feel good"
- Keep the one quality session per week even in taper; just shorter
- Sleep and eating are training — treat them as such in the final 3 weeks

## Shin Splint Protocol (standing rule)

Any shin soreness at or above 2/10 during or after a run:
1. Skip the next quality session; replace with easy 30 min
2. Report to coach before resuming threshold/interval work
3. If soreness persists 3+ days: pause all running, refer to physio
4. Do not "run through it" — every week of early intervention saves 3 weeks of forced rest
